Analyzing Sales Performance, Product Trends, and Geographic Strongholds for Parch & Posey

Problem Statement / Goal:
This analysis explores sales data for Parch & Posey, a paper company, to understand revenue drivers, customer engagement patterns, and channel effectiveness. The goal is to uncover opportunities for geographic expansion, optimize product mix, and develop targeted marketing strategies to maximize revenue growth.

Key Insights:
✪ The "Standard" Dominance: Standard paper is the clear revenue leader at $9.6M, significantly outperforming Glossy and Poster stocks. This indicates a "utility-first" customer base rather than a creative or marketing-heavy one.
✪ Geographic Stronghold (Northeast): The Northeast region generates more than double the revenue ($7.7M) of the Midwest ($3M). There is a massive untapped or underperforming market in the central US.
✪ Channel Dependency: Direct traffic is the undisputed king of web events (5,298). However, "Organic" and "Social" (Twitter/Facebook) are lagging behind, suggesting the brand relies heavily on existing name recognition rather than digital discovery.
✪ Sales Rep Pareto Effect: A few "All-Stars" like Earlie Schleusner ($1.09M) and Tia Amato ($1.01M) are carrying the revenue load. There is a significant performance gap between the top tier and the rest of the sales reps.

Recommendations:
✪ Midwest Expansion Strategy: Assign one of the "Revenue All-Stars" to lead a dedicated Midwest growth pod to replicate the success found in the Northeast.
✪ Digital Marketing Pivot: With conversion rates at a healthy 76%, the bottleneck isn't the website—it's the top-of-funnel traffic. Increase spend in Adwords and Social to diversify away from purely "Direct" traffic.
✪ Upsell "Poster" Stock to Top Clients: Major clients like EOG Resources and Mosaic are already high spenders. Since Poster stock is currently the lowest earner, create bundled "Standard + Poster" packages to increase Average Order Value (AOV).
✪ Sales Mentorship Program: Pair top performers like Earlie Schleusner and Tia Amato with lower-performing reps to share best practices and close the performance gap.
